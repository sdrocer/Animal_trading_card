Eurasian lynx

The name lynx derived from the Indo-European root "leuk" (light brightness) which refers to the luminescence of the animal´s eyes.

  • Scientific Name: Lynx lynx
  • Length of the animal: 80 to 130 cm
  • Average weight: 21.6 kg (males) / 18.1 kg (females)
  • Average Lifespan: 13 years
  • Habitat: Temperate and boreal forests

The Eurasian Lynx is considered the largest member of it´s genus. It has been a target of poaching and illegal trade for decades, however due to the implementation of strict fur-export regulations this threat has declined significantly.
